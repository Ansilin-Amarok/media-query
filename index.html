Ahmedabad: The 2023 wolves census conducted by the Gujarat forest department revealed that the wolves currently inhabit a total area of 2,217.66 square kilometres across 13 districts of the state. The population of the wolves was estimated to be 222. According to the 2023 wolf population estimation, the highest number of wolves, 80, were recorded in Bhavnagar district. The govt stated that to protect the wolf species, the Gujarat Ecological Education and Research (GEER) Foundation has created maps using advanced technologies like remote sensing and GIS. These maps help to identify major habitats and important corridors for wolves in the state, especially in these 13 districts.
